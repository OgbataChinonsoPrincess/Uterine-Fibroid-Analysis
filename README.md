## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive healthcare analytics case study on uterine fibroids using a reconstructed synthetic dataset based on the published research article:

> **A Five-Year Survey of Uterine Fibroids at a Nigerian Tertiary Hospital**
> *A. D. Isah et al.*
> *Open Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology*

The original publication reported aggregated summary statistics rather than patient-level records. To facilitate practical data analysis and visualization, a statistically faithful synthetic dataset comprising **198 patient records** was reconstructed to closely mirror the distributions reported in the study.

Using **Microsoft Excel**, **SQL**, and **Power BI**, this project demonstrates an end-to-end healthcare analytics workflow, from data validation and exploration to dashboard development and clinical insight generation.

> **Disclaimer:** This is a reconstructed synthetic dataset created solely for educational purposes, portfolio development, SQL practice, and healthcare analytics. It does **not** contain real patient records.

# 🌍 Why This Project Matters

Uterine fibroids are among the most common benign gynecological tumors affecting women of reproductive age and are a leading cause of abnormal uterine bleeding, pelvic pain, infertility, and surgical intervention worldwide.

Healthcare analytics plays an important role in transforming clinical data into actionable insights that support:

- Evidence-based clinical decision-making
- Hospital quality improvement initiatives
- Women's sexual and reproductive health research
- Resource allocation and service planning
- Data-driven healthcare policy development

This project illustrates how healthcare data can be transformed into meaningful visualizations that support better understanding of disease patterns, treatment approaches, and postoperative outcomes.

# 📈 Key Findings

## 👩 Patient Demographics

- **Total Patients:** 198
- **Most affected age group:** 30–39 years
- **Patients aged 30–39 years:** 94 (47.5%)

---

## 🩺 Clinical Presentation

- Menorrhagia was the most common presenting complaint.
- **76 patients (38.4%)** presented primarily with menorrhagia.

---

## 🧬 Fibroid Characteristics

- Intramural fibroids were the most frequently observed anatomical subtype.
- **81 patients (40.9%)** had intramural fibroids.

---

## 🏥 Surgical Management

- Myomectomy was the predominant surgical procedure.
- **165 patients (83.3%)** underwent myomectomy, reflecting a preference for fertility-preserving management.

---

## ❤️ Postoperative Outcomes

- Patients with postoperative complications: **57**
- Overall complication rate: **28.8%**
- Anaemia was the most frequently documented postoperative complication (**41 cases**).

--- 

# 🔍 Key Insights

## 👩 Insight 1: Age Distribution

Women aged **30–39 years** accounted for **47.5%** of all reconstructed cases, making this the most affected age group. This finding suggests that uterine fibroids requiring surgical intervention were most common during the peak reproductive years, when preserving fertility is often an important clinical consideration.

---

## 🩸 Insight 2: Clinical Presentation

Menorrhagia was the most frequently reported presenting complaint, affecting **76 patients (38.4%)**. This highlights abnormal uterine bleeding as the leading reason for hospital presentation and surgical management among patients in the study.

---

## 🧬 Insight 3: Fibroid Characteristics

Intramural fibroids were the predominant anatomical subtype, accounting for **81 patients (40.9%)**. Submucosal and subserosal fibroids were also common, while cervical fibroids were relatively rare.

---

## 🏥 Insight 4: Surgical Management

Myomectomy accounted for **83.3%** of all surgical procedures, demonstrating a strong preference for fertility-preserving treatment. This finding is consistent with the relatively young age profile of the patient population.

---

## ❤️ Insight 5: Postoperative Outcomes

Although the majority of patients experienced no postoperative complications, **anaemia** was the most frequently documented complication, representing **41 of the 57 recorded postoperative complications (71.9%)**. This underscores the importance of perioperative blood management and postoperative monitoring.
